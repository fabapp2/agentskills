# Final Report — Required Structure

Produce the final report with the sections below, in order. Keep prose plain
and concise. Every claim must be traceable to an entry in the decision log.

---

## 1. Executive summary
2–4 sentences. The decision, the recommendation, and the single most
important reason. Note that this is a structured analysis, not professional
advice.

## 2. Decision to be made
One sentence restating the decision.

## 3. User objective
What success looks like, in the user's own words.

## 4. Facts gathered
Bulleted list. Each fact tagged with its source (user statement, document,
system). No invented facts.

## 5. Options considered
Numbered list. For each option: a one-line description, then upsides,
downsides, dependencies, and qualitative effort/cost. Include the
"do nothing / status quo" option if it is viable.

## 6. Decision criteria
Bulleted list of the criteria the user agreed to in Phase 3, each with a
short note on how it was applied.

## 7. Risks and implications
For each material risk, repeat the five-part explanation:

- **What** the risk is
- **Why** it matters
- **What could happen** if ignored
- **How** to reduce or investigate it
- **Expert review needed:** yes/no — and what kind

## 8. Assumptions
Bulleted list. Each entry **explicitly labeled as an assumption**. Note
whether the recommendation would change if the assumption turned out to be
wrong.

## 9. Open questions
Items that remain unknown. For each, note which option or criterion it
affects, and how the user could resolve it.

## 10. Recommended decision
The recommendation in one or two sentences. State the confidence level
(low / medium / high) and what would raise it.

## 11. Rationale
A short narrative that walks from objective → criteria → facts → tradeoffs
→ chosen option. No new information here — only synthesis of what is already
in the log.

## 12. Traceability table

| Recommendation element | Linked objective | Supporting fact(s) | Risks addressed | Criteria satisfied | Tradeoffs accepted |
| ---------------------- | ---------------- | ------------------ | --------------- | ------------------ | ------------------ |
| <claim>                | <objective>      | <fact ids>         | <risk ids>      | <criterion ids>    | <tradeoff>         |

Every row must have non-empty entries in every column, or the claim does not
belong in the recommendation.

## 13. Follow-up actions
Concrete next steps with suggested owners and timing.

## 14. Expert review items
Items the user should review with a licensed or specialized professional
before acting. For each, name the kind of expert and the specific questions
to ask.

---

**Closing reminder to include verbatim:**

> This report is a structured analysis of what you told me, not professional
> advice. Items flagged for expert review should be confirmed with a
> qualified professional before you act on them.
